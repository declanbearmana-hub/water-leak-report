On a computer, move the map by clicking and dragging; the pointer remains a normal arrow until an editing tool is active. Choose a utility from the always-visible toolbar inside the map. Its correct colour is selected automatically and line drawing starts immediately. On a touchscreen, tap points to draw the utility line, then use the large Finish control inside the map. Use Cancel to abandon the current drawing and return to normal map movement. On a computer, right-click finishes a line or measurement and Esc cancels the active tool. Double-tap also finishes multi-point work on touchscreens. Drag with one finger and pinch with two fingers to zoom. Use Freehand Sketch to draw directly over the map using the selected utility colour and line width. Use the floating Finish or Cancel controls when finished. Draw Utility also opens the pen, marker, highlighter, line and shape tools. While drawing a utility line, click or tap a hydrant, valve or other symbol to attach the line exactly to its centre. Nearby line points also snap automatically to symbols. With Auto Finish When Connected enabled, the line finishes automatically as soon as its second or later point connects to a symbol, preventing accidental extra segments. Hover over a line, freehand sketch, shape or symbol and click or tap it to open its controls, including colour, move, resize, rotate, duplicate, Add Label and individual delete options. Labels automatically attach to the selected item. Existing labels can be clicked for edit, resize and remove controls.
